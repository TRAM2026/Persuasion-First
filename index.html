The new front door

Google decides who ranks. AI decides who gets recommended. Those are different games.

There is a shift happening to local and professional businesses right now, and most owners haven't seen it yet — because it's invisible from the inside.

People have stopped typing keywords and started asking questions. "Best med spa near me." "Do I have a case worth taking?" "Who do I call for this?" The AI answers the way a trusted friend would: with a few names, stated with total confidence.

The businesses being named aren't better. They're legible — described consistently across the web, structured the way machines read, cited by sources the engines already trust. That layer didn't exist two years ago, so almost nobody built it. The ones who build it first are hard to displace, because AI reinforces the sources it already trusts.
